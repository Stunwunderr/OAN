TRADE PROCESS

At OAN Nature Export Limited (ONEL), we offer buyers attractive sales terms supported by the appropriate payment methods. Our international shipping agreement is either Ex Works (EXW), Free On Board (FOB) or Cost, Insurance & Fraught (CIF) depending on the buyer's preference. The terms of this agreement are based on the International Commerce Terminology (INCOTERMS) of 2020 developed by the international chamber of commerce.



EX WORKS(EXW)

Under this term, the seller(exporter) makes the products available at a designated location and the buyer incurred transportation. Once the buyer collect the product, the buyer assumes all other responsibilities including loading the goods on to a vehicle (even though the seller may be better place to do this); For all export procedures; for onward transport and for all costs arising after collection of the goods the seller (Exporter) may still need to be involved in the export reporting and clearance processes, and cannot realistically leave this to the buyer. Although the seller is not obliged to know the goods, if the seller does so, the buyers bears the risk.



FREE ON BOARD(FOB)

The term specifies that the seller (exporter) assume all the cost up to the shipping point. This cost includes payments for the transportation of the goods to the port of shipment plus loading costs. With the FOB type of shipping agreement, the seller has fulfilled obligation and delivery is accomplished when the goods are loaded on the vessel nominated by the buyer of the named port of shipment, while the buyer (importer) pays the cost of marine freight transport, insurance, unloading and transportation from the originating port to the final destination.



COST, INSURANCE & FREIGHT (CIF)

With the CIF type of shipping agreement, the seller (exporter) is responsible for all costs up until the goods have reached the named port of destination. Under this term CIF- Cost, Insurance and Freight, the seller (exporter) has responsibility for the cost of the goods in transit, providing insurance and paying freight charges to move the goods to a destination chosen by the buyer. From the point of delivery at the destination, the buyer (exporter) assumes responsibility for unloading changes and any further shipping cost to a final destination.
